# Charter ISP Learning Plan Generator

You are a drafting partner for the individualized learning plan a charter school independent study program expects, common in states like California where a family enrolls a homeschool-style program through a public charter rather than filing directly as a private homeschool. This kind of plan, sometimes called a personalized learning plan, gets built around the state's academic content standards and the child's individual abilities, interests, and learning style, but it is developed together with an assigned credentialed teacher, not decided unilaterally by the family. Build this as the strong first draft you bring into that meeting, not a document you file on your own.

Draft a learning plan for [CHILD_NAME_OR_INITIALS], at the [GRADE_LEVEL] level. If you know the specific charter or independent study program, name it in [CHARTER_OR_ISP_NAME?]. Describe the subjects and goals for this period in [SUBJECTS_AND_GOALS]. If there's a known learning style or strong interest area, note it in [LEARNING_STYLE_OR_INTERESTS?].

1. Organize [SUBJECTS_AND_GOALS] into a standard subject-by-subject plan, English language arts, math, science, social studies, and any electives, stating a specific goal for each subject rather than a general intention to cover it.

2. Tie each subject's goal loosely to grade-level academic content standards, in the general language a credentialed teacher would recognize, and flag plainly that this connection should be confirmed and refined by the assigned teacher, not treated as an official standards alignment.

3. If [LEARNING_STYLE_OR_INTERESTS?] was provided, note one place in the plan where materials or approach could reasonably flex around it, since a personalized plan is meant to account for how this specific learner engages, not just what content gets covered.

4. Add a placeholder section for the periodic check-in most independent study programs require, since families typically meet with their assigned teacher on a set cycle, commonly monthly, to review progress and work samples against this plan.

Close by stating plainly that this draft is meant to speed up, not replace, the collaborative planning meeting with your assigned credentialed teacher or education specialist, since most independent study programs require that teacher's involvement and sign-off before a learning plan is official.
