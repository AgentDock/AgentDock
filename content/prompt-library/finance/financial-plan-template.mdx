# Financial Plan Template

You are an experienced financial planning specialist who has helped individuals, families, startups, and established businesses build actionable financial plans. You understand that a financial plan is a living roadmap connecting where someone stands today with where they want to be in the future. You translate complex financial concepts into clear language, adapt recommendations to different risk profiles, and create plans that people actually follow.

I need you to create a comprehensive financial plan based on the following information.

Plan type: [PLAN_TYPE:select:personal,business,startup,nonprofit]

Planning horizon: [PLANNING_HORIZON:select:1 year,3 years,5 years,10 years]

Current financial situation: [CURRENT_FINANCIAL_SITUATION]

Income sources and amounts: [INCOME_SOURCES]

Primary financial goals: [FINANCIAL_GOALS]

Risk tolerance: [RISK_TOLERANCE:select:conservative,moderate,aggressive]

Monthly or annual expenses overview: [EXPENSE_OVERVIEW]

Existing debts and obligations: [EXISTING_DEBTS?]

Current investment portfolio or savings: [INVESTMENT_PORTFOLIO?]

Life stage or business stage context: [LIFE_STAGE?]

Tax considerations or jurisdiction: [TAX_CONTEXT?]

Begin the plan with an executive summary that captures the current financial position, the primary goals, and the overall strategy in a few concise paragraphs. Give the reader immediate clarity on where they stand and where the plan aims to take them over the selected planning horizon.

Next, create a net worth assessment based on the financial information provided. List all assets and liabilities, calculate total net worth, and note any concerns the numbers reveal. If you need to make assumptions because certain details were not provided, state those assumptions clearly.

Follow with a cash flow analysis. Map income against expenses on a monthly basis, identify the surplus or deficit, and highlight recurring expenses that consume a disproportionate share of income. For business plans, break this down by revenue streams and operational cost categories.

Build a goal-setting section that translates each financial goal into a specific, time-bound target with a dollar amount attached. Calculate the monthly or annual savings rate required to reach each goal. If the goals exceed what current income allows, rank them by priority and recommend a phased approach.

Create a budgeting strategy section that recommends a framework based on the plan type. For personal plans, consider the 50/30/20 rule, zero-based budgeting, or envelope-style allocation. For business or startup plans, organize the budget around categories such as payroll, marketing, technology, and growth investment. Show allocations as both dollar amounts and percentages.

Develop an investment strategy tailored to the stated risk tolerance. For conservative profiles, emphasize capital preservation with fixed-income instruments and diversified index funds. For moderate profiles, build a balanced mix of equities and bonds. For aggressive profiles, include growth equities and alternative investments while explaining volatility trade-offs. Provide specific allocation percentages. For business plans, address reinvestment priorities and fundraising considerations.

Include a risk management section evaluating protection against unexpected events like job loss, disability, or business interruption. Recommend insurance coverage that may be missing and explain how it fits into the overall plan.

Add a debt management section if debts were provided. Analyze interest rates and repayment schedules, recommend a payoff strategy using either the avalanche or snowball method, and calculate the projected payoff timeline.

Include a tax planning section identifying opportunities to reduce tax burden. For personal plans, cover retirement contributions, tax-loss harvesting, or deduction optimization. For business plans, address entity structure and estimated quarterly obligations.

Create a milestone schedule that breaks the planning horizon into checkpoints with specific metrics to measure, such as net worth targets, debt reduction, and portfolio value. Recommend a review frequency.

Conclude with an action plan listing five to eight concrete steps to take in the next 30 days. Each item should be specific enough that the reader knows exactly what to do, which accounts to open, and which conversations to start.

Format the plan with clear section headings and tables for numerical comparisons, organized so any section can be referenced independently.
