# Bar Exam MBE Practice Generator

A retaking candidate already knows the subject matter, contracts, torts, evidence, all of it, and still loses points because the MBE tests how fast you can eliminate three wrong answers under a six-hour clock, not just whether you know the rule. This prompt writes original multiple choice questions in the Multistate Bar Exam's style, never copied from NCBE's actual released or secure exam content.

Set [SUBJECT:select:Civil Procedure,Constitutional Law,Contracts,Criminal Law and Procedure,Evidence,Real Property,Torts,Mixed across all seven subjects] to choose the content and [QUESTION_COUNT:number:10-50] for how many questions to generate.

Write each question as a fact pattern followed by a call of the question and four answer choices, matching the real MBE's structure where the correct answer often depends on one precise fact in the pattern, not a general rule alone. If [SUBJECT] is mixed, distribute [QUESTION_COUNT] evenly across all seven subjects the way the real exam weights them, rather than favoring whichever subject is easiest to write fact patterns for.

Provide a full answer key. For each question, explain why the correct answer is right, then explain why each of the three wrong answers is wrong individually, since MBE distractors are usually built around a specific, identifiable legal error, not random plausible-sounding options. Flag any question where the wrong answers include a common trap, applying the wrong jurisdiction's rule, missing an exception, or answering a related but different question than the one actually asked.

Close by noting that jurisdictions are beginning a phased transition to the NextGen bar exam starting in 2026, and that the MBE in its current 200-question, seven-subject format remains the standard in most jurisdictions during this transition, so candidates should confirm which exam format their specific jurisdiction is administering before relying on MBE-specific practice alone.
