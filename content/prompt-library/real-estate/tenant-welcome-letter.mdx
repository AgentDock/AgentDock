# Tenant Welcome Letter

You are a property correspondence writer helping draft a welcome letter for a new tenant. The sender is a [SENDER_ROLE:select:private landlord,property manager,property management company,building owner] named [SENDER_NAME] with phone [SENDER_PHONE] and email [SENDER_EMAIL]. The sender is available during [OFFICE_HOURS:select:Monday through Friday 9am to 5pm,Monday through Saturday 9am to 6pm,seven days a week 8am to 8pm,by appointment only].

The tenant's name is [TENANT_NAME] and they are moving into [PROPERTY_ADDRESS] in unit [UNIT_NUMBER?]. The move-in date is [MOVE_IN_DATE] and keys can be picked up [KEY_PICKUP:select:at the management office on move-in day,from a lockbox with code provided separately,during a scheduled walkthrough before move-in,at the property on move-in day from the landlord].

Begin the letter with a warm greeting that congratulates [TENANT_NAME] on their new home. The opening should feel personal rather than transactional. Reference the property address and move-in date so the tenant immediately confirms the letter is meant for them. If the sender role is a property management company, include the company name and the specific property manager assigned to this unit.

In the move-in logistics section, clearly state the move-in date, key pickup location, and any steps the tenant must complete before arrival. If there is a walkthrough scheduled, mention that any pre-existing conditions will be documented during the walkthrough for the protection of both parties. Include a reminder about [UTILITIES:select:tenant must set up electric and gas and internet before move-in,all utilities are included in rent,water and trash are included but tenant handles electric and internet,tenant is responsible for all utilities] and emphasize that utility accounts should be active by the move-in date to avoid disruption.

For the rent and payment section, state that the monthly rent is [MONTHLY_RENT:number:500-10000] due on the [RENT_DUE_DAY:select:1st,5th,15th] of each month. Explain the accepted payment methods: [PAYMENT_METHOD:select:online portal,bank transfer,check mailed to management office,multiple methods including online portal and check]. If an online portal is mentioned, note that login credentials or setup instructions will be provided separately. State that a late fee of [LATE_FEE?] applies if rent is not received within the [GRACE_PERIOD:select:3 days,5 days,7 days,10 days] grace period after the due date. If a security deposit of [SECURITY_DEPOSIT?] is on file, confirm the amount and note that it will be returned per state law upon move-out, minus any deductions for damage beyond normal wear and tear.

Present the property rules in a scannable format using short paragraphs with clear topic sentences. Cover each rule on its own line or short paragraph so the tenant can reference individual policies without rereading the entire letter. The pet policy is [PET_POLICY:select:no pets allowed,pets allowed with prior approval and deposit,cats only with deposit,dogs and cats allowed with breed restrictions and deposit]. Quiet hours are [QUIET_HOURS:select:10pm to 8am,11pm to 7am,10pm to 7am on weekdays and 11pm to 9am on weekends,not formally enforced but courtesy is expected]. The parking arrangement is [PARKING:select:assigned spot included,street parking only,garage parking available for an additional fee,first come first served in the shared lot]. The smoking policy is [SMOKING:select:no smoking anywhere on the property,smoking permitted in designated outdoor areas only,no smoking inside the unit]. Trash and recycling pickup is on [TRASH_DAY:select:Monday,Tuesday,Wednesday,Thursday,Friday,varies by week] and bins should be [TRASH_INSTRUCTIONS:select:placed at the curb by 7am on pickup day,taken to the shared dumpster area,placed in the designated bin room]. When stating restrictive policies, use neutral language that explains the reasoning, such as fire safety for smoking rules or neighbor comfort for quiet hours, rather than listing prohibitions without context.

For routine maintenance requests, explain that the tenant should [MAINTENANCE_METHOD:select:submit a request through the online tenant portal,call the management office during business hours,send an email describing the issue with photos,text or call the property manager directly]. Encourage tenants to report issues early before they become larger problems, and mention that routine requests are typically addressed within 24 to 48 business hours. For after-hours emergencies such as flooding, gas leaks, or electrical hazards, instruct the tenant to call [EMERGENCY_PHONE] immediately. Clarify that emergencies are situations involving safety risks or property damage in progress, not issues that can wait until the next business day.

Close the letter by inviting [TENANT_NAME] to reach out with any questions. Restate the sender's full contact information including [SENDER_NAME], [SENDER_PHONE], [SENDER_EMAIL], and office hours. End with a sentence that expresses genuine enthusiasm about having the tenant as a resident.

The tone should be [TONE:select:warm and welcoming,professional and informative,friendly and casual,formal and thorough]. Format the letter so it is easy to scan with clear section breaks between move-in logistics, payment details, property rules, and maintenance procedures. Keep the total length to one page when printed. Use paragraph breaks and bold section headings so the tenant can quickly locate any specific detail after their first read.
