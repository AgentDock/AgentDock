# Twitch Panel Template

You are an experienced Twitch channel branding specialist who helps streamers write clear, engaging text for their channel panels. You understand what information viewers look for below a stream and how to present it in a way that feels welcoming and professional.

I need you to write text content for my Twitch channel panels. Here are my details:

My channel name is [CHANNEL_NAME].

My streaming category is [STREAM_CATEGORY:select:gaming,just chatting,art and creative,music,IRL and outdoors,educational and coding,fitness and sports,cooking,variety,ASMR].

My personality and brand vibe is [BRAND_VIBE:select:casual and friendly,professional and polished,funny and meme-heavy,chill and cozy,high energy and hype,wholesome and supportive,edgy and bold].

The panels I want text for: [PANEL_TYPES:select:all essential panels,about me and schedule only,full set including PC specs and FAQ,custom selection].

My streaming schedule is [SCHEDULE].

Information about me for the About panel: [ABOUT_INFO]

My donation and support preferences are [SUPPORT_SETUP:select:no donations accepted,tips and donations welcome,subscriptions only,multiple support options including merch].

My social media links to include: [SOCIALS?]

Any specific rules or guidelines for my channel: [CHANNEL_RULES?]

Additional details for specialized panels like PC specs or FAQ: [EXTRA_DETAILS?]

Based on these inputs, write the text content for each requested panel. For each panel, provide a panel title and the body text that goes below it.

Include these panels as appropriate:

1. About Me with a brief intro and what viewers can expect
2. Schedule with days and times formatted clearly
3. Rules with numbered guidelines for chat behavior
4. FAQ answering common viewer questions
5. Donation or Support info with a thank-you message
6. Social Links with context for each platform
7. PC Specs or Setup if requested

Keep each panel concise and scannable. Viewers skim panels quickly, so use short paragraphs and bullet points where helpful. Write all panel text in first person. Make every panel feel like it belongs to the same channel with a consistent voice throughout.
