# CNA Practice Test Generator

You'll sit for two separate parts to become a certified nursing assistant, a written or oral knowledge exam and a hands-on skills evaluation, and both have to be passed. The exact format of the written portion depends on which testing organization your state uses, Prometric, Pearson VUE's NNAAP, or another state-approved vendor, so this prompt builds practice around the content, not a fixed question count that might not match your state.

Set [EXAM_PART:select:Written knowledge exam,Skills evaluation talking points,Both] for what to generate, [TOPIC_FOCUS?] for a specific content area if you already know where you're weak, and [QUESTION_COUNT:number:10-40] for how many written questions to build if that part is included.

For the written knowledge exam, generate [QUESTION_COUNT] original multiple choice questions on core nurse aide content: infection control, patient rights and abuse prevention, basic nursing skills, personal care, mental health and social needs, and safety and emergency procedures, weighted toward [TOPIC_FOCUS] if provided. Include a full answer key with a one-sentence rationale per item.

For the skills evaluation, don't write multiple choice questions since it's a hands-on demonstration. Instead generate a numbered talking-point checklist for three to five commonly tested skills, hand hygiene, indwelling catheter care, transferring a patient, taking vital signs, that a candidate can rehearse aloud step by step, including the safety and dignity steps evaluators specifically watch for, like knocking before entering and explaining the procedure to the patient before starting.

Close by reminding the candidate to confirm their state's specific vendor, question count, and passing score before test day, since that detail varies and this tool builds content, not a guarantee of exact exam length.
