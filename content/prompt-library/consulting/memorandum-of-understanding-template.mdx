# Memorandum of Understanding Template

You are a legal and business writing expert who specializes in drafting memoranda of understanding for organizations of all sizes. You have prepared MOUs for government agencies, Fortune 500 partnerships, nonprofit collaborations, and startup joint ventures. Your documents are known for being thorough yet readable, balancing legal precision with clear language that non-lawyers can follow. You understand that an MOU is not a binding contract but a formal expression of mutual intent that sets the foundation for a successful working relationship.

I need you to draft a memorandum of understanding between the following parties.

The first party is [PARTY_1_NAME], represented by [PARTY_1_REPRESENTATIVE] with the title [PARTY_1_TITLE]. Their organization can be described as: [PARTY_1_DESCRIPTION, e.g., "a state university with 25,000 enrolled students and an annual research budget of $180 million"].

The second party is [PARTY_2_NAME], represented by [PARTY_2_REPRESENTATIVE] with the title [PARTY_2_TITLE]. Their organization can be described as: [PARTY_2_DESCRIPTION, e.g., "a biotech startup focused on developing sustainable agricultural solutions"].

If there is a third party involved, include them here: [PARTY_3_INFO?]

The type of understanding being established is [MOU_TYPE:select:Business Partnership,Joint Venture,Research Collaboration,Government Agreement,Nonprofit Partnership,Service Agreement,Technology Transfer,Educational Collaboration,Community Development Initiative,Interagency Cooperation].

The purpose of this memorandum of understanding is: [MOU_PURPOSE, e.g., "to establish a framework for joint research into drought-resistant crop varieties, share laboratory facilities, and co-publish findings over a three-year period"].

The key terms and commitments each party is agreeing to are: [KEY_TERMS, e.g., "Party 1 provides lab space and graduate research assistants. Party 2 provides proprietary seed genetics and funding for field trials. Both parties share intellectual property rights equally on joint discoveries"].

The intended duration of this agreement is [DURATION:select:6 months,1 year,2 years,3 years,5 years,Open-ended with annual review,Project-based until completion].

The financial arrangements or resource commitments include: [FINANCIAL_TERMS?, e.g., "Party 2 will contribute $500,000 annually for research costs. Party 1 waives facility usage fees. Shared costs for conference travel and publication fees are split 60/40"].

Confidentiality requirements for this arrangement are: [CONFIDENTIALITY?, e.g., "All shared research data, proprietary methods, and unpublished findings must remain confidential for 24 months after the MOU expires"].

The governing law or jurisdiction for this understanding is: [GOVERNING_LAW?, e.g., "State of California, United States"]

Any additional clauses or special provisions to include: [ADDITIONAL_CLAUSES?, e.g., "Force majeure clause, data protection compliance with GDPR, annual performance review meetings"]

Draft a complete memorandum of understanding with the following sections. Write in clear, professional language that both legal teams and operational staff can follow.

Begin with a preamble identifying all parties by full legal names, addresses, and the effective date. State the shared intention and spirit of cooperation.

Write a purpose and scope section explaining why the parties are entering this understanding, what activities fall within its boundaries, and what outcomes they expect. Be precise about what is included and what falls outside scope.

Create a roles and responsibilities section organized by party. List specific obligations, deliverables, timelines, and resources each party contributes. Define measurable actions rather than vague commitments.

Include a timeline and milestones section mapping key phases from start to finish. Identify dates for deliverables, review points, and decision gates. Note dependencies where one party's progress relies on another completing a prior step.

Draft a financial arrangements section covering funding commitments, cost-sharing formulas, reimbursement procedures, and budget oversight. If no money changes hands, state that explicitly and describe in-kind contributions.

Write a confidentiality and intellectual property section defining what information is confidential, how long obligations last, who owns IP created during the collaboration, and how ownership disputes get resolved. Address both existing IP and new jointly created IP.

Include a communication and governance section establishing points of contact, meeting frequency, reporting requirements, and the escalation path for disagreements.

Create a dispute resolution section with a graduated process: direct negotiation first, then mediation, then a final resolution method. Specify where proceedings take place and who bears costs.

Write a term, amendment, and termination section stating the effective date, duration, process for amendments, and conditions for early termination. Specify notice periods and obligations that survive after the MOU ends.

Include general provisions covering the non-binding nature of the document, clarification that no employer-employee relationship is created, force majeure conditions, assignment restrictions, and notice delivery methods.

Close with a signature block including spaces for each party's authorized representative name, title, organization, signature, and date.

Format the document with clear numbered sections and subsections. Avoid unnecessary legal jargon while protecting all parties.

This memorandum of understanding template is for informational and educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. MOU requirements vary significantly by jurisdiction, organization type, and purpose. Before using any MOU in a real business situation, consult with a qualified attorney to ensure the document complies with applicable laws and adequately protects your interests.
