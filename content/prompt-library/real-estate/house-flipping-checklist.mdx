# House Flipping Checklist

You are a real estate investment advisor who specializes in residential property flips. I need a complete house flipping checklist customized to my project so I can track every phase from acquisition through resale.

The property I am targeting is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,duplex,triplex,fourplex,condominium,fixer-upper bungalow] located in [LOCATION]. It was built in [YEAR_BUILT:number:1900-2026] and has approximately [SQUARE_FEET:number:400-10000] square feet. The property is currently [CONDITION:select:livable but outdated,needs moderate renovation,needs major renovation,uninhabitable or condemned,foreclosure as-is].

My purchase price is [PURCHASE_PRICE:number:10000-5000000] dollars and my total renovation budget is [RENOVATION_BUDGET:number:5000-1000000] dollars. I estimate the after-repair value at [ARV:number:50000-10000000] dollars. My financing method is [FINANCING:select:cash purchase,hard money loan,conventional mortgage,private lender,partnership or syndication,home equity line of credit].

My experience level with flipping is [EXPERIENCE:select:first flip ever,have done 1 to 3 flips,experienced with 4 to 10 flips,professional flipper with 10 or more]. My target timeline from purchase to sale is [TIMELINE:select:under 3 months,3 to 6 months,6 to 9 months,9 to 12 months,over 12 months]. My exit strategy is [EXIT_STRATEGY:select:sell on open market after renovation,sell to another investor,rent then sell later,wholesale the contract,live in then sell].

My primary renovation focus areas include [FOCUS_AREAS?]. I plan to handle the work through [WORK_APPROACH:select:hiring a general contractor,managing individual subcontractors myself,doing most work as DIY,a combination of DIY and hired labor].

Generate a phase-by-phase house flipping checklist covering these stages. Start with pre-acquisition due diligence including market research, comparable sales analysis, the 70% rule calculation, title search, lien verification, and property inspection findings. Include a deal analysis summary showing purchase price, estimated rehab, holding costs, selling costs, and projected profit.

Next cover the acquisition and closing phase with tasks for securing financing, reviewing contracts, obtaining insurance, and setting up utilities. Then create the renovation planning section listing scope of work creation, permit applications, contractor selection, material sourcing, and a draw schedule tied to milestones.

Follow with the active renovation phase organized by system: structural and foundation, roofing and exterior, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, interior finishes, kitchen, bathrooms, flooring, paint, and landscaping. For each system include inspection checkpoints and quality control steps.

Add a pre-listing preparation section covering final inspections, cleaning, staging recommendations, photography, and pricing strategy. Finish with the sale and closing phase including offer evaluation, negotiation points, appraisal preparation, and final settlement tasks.

For each checklist item, include a status field, estimated cost, actual cost, responsible party, and target completion date. Flag items that carry the highest risk of budget overruns given the property age and condition. Format the entire checklist as a printable tracking document with running cost totals at each phase so I can monitor budget against actual spending throughout the flip.
