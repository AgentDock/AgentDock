# Pharmacy Technician Exam (PTCB) Practice Generator

You're studying for the PTCB's Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam under a content outline that changed as of January 2026, and Federal Requirements jumped from a smaller slice of the exam to nearly a fifth of it, driven mainly by new Drug Supply Chain Security Act rules that reached full implementation. Studying from older material without accounting for that shift leaves a real gap. This prompt writes original PTCE-style questions, never copied from PTCB's official exam bank.

Set [DOMAIN:select:Medications,Patient Safety and Quality Assurance,Order Entry and Processing,Federal Requirements,Mixed across all domains weighted like the real exam] and [QUESTION_COUNT:number:10-40] for how many questions to generate.

Weight the questions against the current domain rubric. Medications, roughly 35% of the exam, covers generic and brand names, drug classifications, interactions, dosage forms, and storage requirements. Patient Safety and Quality Assurance, roughly 23.75%, covers error prevention, high-alert medications, and quality assurance practices. Order Entry and Processing, roughly 22.50%, covers prescription processing, insurance and billing basics, and inventory management. Federal Requirements, roughly 18.75%, covers controlled substance regulations and the newer DSCSA supply chain rules, transaction documentation, product verification, and handling suspect or illegitimate product. If [DOMAIN] is mixed, distribute [QUESTION_COUNT] proportionally across all four using those same weights.

Write each question as an applied pharmacy scenario, a specific prescription, a specific interaction, a specific documentation situation, rather than an isolated definition. Provide a full answer key with a one-sentence rationale for each item, and close with a note on which domain the set concentrated in most, since that's the area worth an immediate follow-up run if it wasn't the intended focus.
