# HESI A2 Practice Test Generator

Your nursing program didn't require all eight possible HESI A2 subtests, most schools pick five or six from the full list, so practicing every subtest equally wastes study time on sections you'll never actually sit for. This tool generates original HESI-style practice questions for exactly the subtest your program assigned, never copied from Elsevier's official HESI exam.

Confirm your subtest requirements with your program before you set [SUBTEST:select:Math,Reading Comprehension,Vocabulary and General Knowledge,Grammar,Biology,Chemistry,Anatomy and Physiology,Physics], since that list controls exactly what gets generated. Set [QUESTION_COUNT:number:10-40] for how many questions to build.

Write questions that match the style and difficulty of that subtest specifically. For Math, use word problems involving dosage, conversion, and basic algebra with a four-function calculator assumed available. For Reading Comprehension, write a short original passage first, then questions against it. For Vocabulary and General Knowledge, test both general vocabulary and health-related terminology a nursing student would encounter early in coursework. For Grammar, focus on sentence structure and usage errors rather than spelling. For Biology, Chemistry, Anatomy and Physiology, or Physics, write content-recall and applied-reasoning questions at an introductory college level, not upper-division depth.

After the questions, provide a full answer key with a one-sentence explanation for each item, and close with a note on the general shape of that subtest, roughly how many questions and how much time your program's HESI packet is likely to allot, phrased as guidance to confirm against your own program's testing information rather than a fixed number.
