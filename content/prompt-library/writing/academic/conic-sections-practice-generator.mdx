Before completing any square, look only at the coefficients on the x squared term and the y squared term and classify the conic using this test: if one of those two coefficients is zero, it's a parabola. If both coefficients are equal, it's a circle. If both coefficients share the same sign but aren't equal, it's an ellipse. If the two coefficients have opposite signs, it's a hyperbola. State the center or vertex, and any relevant a, b, or r values, directly from that standard form. As a check, expand your standard form equation back out algebraically and confirm it returns to the original general form equation you started with.
