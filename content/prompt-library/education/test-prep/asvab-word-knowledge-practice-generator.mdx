# ASVAB Word Knowledge Practice Generator

Can you spot the closest meaning to a word you've never used in conversation? That's the core skill Word Knowledge tests, either asking for the best synonym directly or asking what a word means as used in a sentence. Alongside Paragraph Comprehension, Word Knowledge forms the verbal half of your AFQT score, combined with Arithmetic Reasoning and Mathematics Knowledge on the math side. A weak vocabulary score here can undercut an otherwise strong AFQT even for someone confident in math.

Build a set on [WORD_LEVEL:select:Everyday vocabulary,Workplace and technical vocabulary,Advanced vocabulary,Mixed difficulty] with [QUESTION_COUNT:number:5-20] questions in [FORMAT:select:Synonym only (choose the closest meaning),In-context (word used in a sentence),Mixed format matching the real test].

For synonym questions, include four answer options where at least two are plausible enough to require real word knowledge, not obvious wrong answers. For in-context questions, write a full sentence using the target word so its meaning has to be inferred from usage, not guessed from the word alone. Provide a full answer key that explains the word's meaning, notes a recognizable root or prefix when one exists, and explains why each wrong option doesn't fit as precisely as the correct answer.
