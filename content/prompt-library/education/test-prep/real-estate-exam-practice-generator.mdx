# Real Estate License Exam Practice Generator

Every state real estate licensing exam runs the same two-part structure underneath, a national portion covering real estate principles that don't change by state line, and a state-specific portion covering the exact statutes, license law, and disclosure rules of wherever you're getting licensed. Studying only the national content and assuming the state portion will take care of itself is the single most common gap candidates run into.

Set [STATE] to the state you're licensing in, [PORTION:select:National portion,State-specific portion,Both] to choose what to generate, and [QUESTION_COUNT:number:10-40] for how many questions to build.

Build the national portion first if selected, covering property ownership, land use controls, valuation and market analysis, financing, contracts, agency relationships, and closing procedures, the core body of real estate principles tested nationwide regardless of [STATE]. Then build the state-specific portion if selected, covering [STATE]'s license law structure, required disclosures, agency and brokerage relationship rules, and any state-specific contract or closing practice differences, noting explicitly that [STATE] law changes over time and this content should be checked against your state's current licensing candidate handbook.

For every question, provide four answer options with one correct response, then a full answer key with a one-sentence explanation of why the correct answer is right and, for the state-specific portion, a citation-style note naming which type of state rule the question is testing, such as agency disclosure requirements or license law violations, rather than a specific statute number that could be outdated.

Close with a short reminder that passing scores and the exact split between national and state questions vary by state, so confirm both against your state's real estate commission before test day.
