# Open House Checklist

You are a senior real estate agent with 15 years of experience hosting successful open houses across every property type and market condition. I need you to build a detailed open house checklist that covers every phase from initial planning through post-event follow-up.

The property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,condominium,luxury estate,multi-family,new construction,vacant land] located in [LOCATION] with [BEDROOMS:number:1-10] bedrooms and [BATHROOMS:number:1-8] bathrooms. The listing price is [LISTING_PRICE] and the total living area is approximately [SQUARE_FEET:number:500-15000] square feet. My role is [AGENT_ROLE:select:listing agent,buyer's agent hosting for exposure,team lead coordinating multiple agents,new agent hosting my first open house]. The open house format will be [FORMAT:select:traditional weekend open house,broker's open or caravan,twilight or evening event,virtual open house with live walkthrough,mega open house with community event]. My target audience is [AUDIENCE:select:first-time home buyers,move-up buyers,investors and flippers,luxury buyers,downsizers and retirees,general mix of all buyer types].

The event is scheduled for [EVENT_DATE] from [START_TIME] to [END_TIME], and I want to begin preparation [PREP_TIMELINE:select:one week before,two weeks before,three days before,same week]. Any special property features or concerns I want highlighted include [SPECIAL_NOTES?].

Please create a comprehensive open house checklist organized into the following phases. For the planning and marketing phase, cover scheduling strategy, MLS and online listing updates, social media promotion, email campaigns to your database, neighborhood door-knocking or flyer distribution, directional signage placement, and coordination with the homeowner on showing expectations.

For property preparation, include deep cleaning priorities, decluttering and depersonalizing guidance, staging recommendations tailored to the property type and target audience, curb appeal improvements, lighting adjustments, temperature and scent considerations, and any repairs or touch-ups that should be completed before the event.

For the day-of hosting phase, detail the arrival and setup timeline, sign-in sheet or digital lead capture setup, property information packets and flyers to have on hand, talking points for the property's best features, how to handle objections or tough questions from visitors, safety and security protocols for the homeowner's belongings, and refreshment or hospitality touches appropriate for the format.

For follow-up after the event, outline the process for organizing leads captured during the open house, recommended timing and messaging for follow-up emails and calls, feedback collection for the seller, social media recap content, and how to convert open house visitors into active clients or scheduled private showings.

For each checklist item, note the recommended completion timeline relative to the event date. Flag the five highest-impact items that most directly influence attendance and lead generation. Format the entire checklist so it can be printed as a day-by-day action plan with checkboxes for each task.
