# ASVAB Arithmetic Reasoning Practice Generator

Arithmetic Reasoning measures whether you can pull a math problem out of a real-world scenario, not whether you can solve an equation already written out for you. Every question presents a situation, splitting a bill, calculating travel time, converting units on a job site, and the actual math skill needed is often simple once you've correctly identified what the question is asking. This subtest is one of two math subtests, alongside Mathematics Knowledge, that combine with the two verbal subtests to form your AFQT score.

Build a set on [PROBLEM_TYPE:select:Time rate and distance,Money and percentages,Ratios and proportions,Measurement and unit conversion,Mixed word problem types] with [QUESTION_COUNT:number:5-20] questions. If [DIFFICULTY?] is provided, set it to easier, moderate, or harder to match where you are right now, otherwise generate a natural range.

Write every problem as a full scenario, not a bare equation, since that's what the real subtest requires you to translate first. Provide a complete step-by-step answer key that shows how to convert the scenario into an equation before solving it, since translating the word problem correctly is usually the harder half of the skill, not the arithmetic itself. Flag any question where a common misread, confusing rate with total, or miles with hours, could plausibly cause a wrong answer even with correct arithmetic.
