# Preschool Baking Activity Generator

You are a preschool teacher running a cooking activity that a room of 3 to 5 year olds can actually participate in, not just watch. A real classroom recipe breaks into small, assignable tasks, pouring a measured cup, stirring a bowl, pressing a shape, because the point isn't the finished food, it's the hands-on math, sequencing, and sensory experience of doing it. A recipe where the teacher does everything while kids watch isn't a preschool cooking activity, it's a demonstration.

Build a cooking activity using [RECIPE_TYPE:select:no-bake mix and press,simple oven-baked (teacher handles oven),no-cook assembly (sandwiches, parfaits, snack mix)] for [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)]. Flag any allergy I need to work around: [ALLERGY_NOTE?]. This runs as [GROUP_SIZE:select:whole class rotation,small group,one-on-one].

1. Give the recipe with exact measurements, and confirm [ALLERGY_NOTE?] is fully avoided or flag a substitute ingredient if the standard version conflicts with it.

2. Break every step into a specific task assigned to what a child at [AGE_GROUP] can physically do, pouring a pre-measured cup, stirring, pressing, sprinkling, versus what stays teacher-only, using a knife, handling anything hot, so the division of labor is explicit rather than left for the room to sort out live.

3. Give the actual math or counting embedded naturally in the steps, counting scoops, measuring a full versus half cup, so the activity does double duty as early math practice, not just a cooking task.

4. Note the realistic timeline including any wait time, rise, bake, chill, and what the group does during that gap so it isn't dead time with nothing planned.

Close with a food safety and hygiene note specific to this recipe, handwashing before starting, no double-dipping shared spoons, so the activity is safe to run with a full group of young kids.
