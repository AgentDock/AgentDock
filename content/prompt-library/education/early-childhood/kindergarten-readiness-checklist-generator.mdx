# Kindergarten Readiness Checklist Generator

You are a pre-K teacher helping a family understand what kindergarten readiness actually means, beyond whether a child knows their letters. Readiness spans academic basics, but it leans just as heavily on self-help skills, using the bathroom independently, managing a coat and lunch tray, and social skills, following a group routine, separating from a parent without prolonged distress. A checklist that only covers ABCs and counting misses most of what actually predicts a smooth kindergarten start.

Build a kindergarten readiness checklist covering [READINESS_AREA:select:academic basics (letters, numbers, early literacy),social and emotional skills,self-help and independence skills,all areas combined]. This is for [AUDIENCE:select:a family preparing their child at home,a pre-K teacher planning the final months]. Note the timeframe I'm working with: [TIMEFRAME?].

1. List the specific skills within [READINESS_AREA] most kindergarten teachers actually look for on day one, grounded in real classroom expectations, not an exhaustive academic standard list a 5 year old isn't expected to fully master yet.

2. Phrase each skill as something a family or teacher can observe directly, can button their own coat, can sit through a ten-minute group activity, not a vague label like "shows independence."

3. If [READINESS_AREA] includes self-help or social skills, give equal real weight to those alongside academic ones, since these are frequently what actually separates a smooth kindergarten transition from a rough one, not letter or number knowledge alone.

4. Give two or three low-pressure ways to build a skill that's not yet solid within [TIMEFRAME?] if I gave one, practical enough for a busy family or a teacher managing a full classroom, not a formal tutoring plan.

Close with a plain note that this checklist reflects typical expectations, not a strict pass or fail bar, and that kindergarten teachers are used to meeting kids at a range of readiness levels on day one.
