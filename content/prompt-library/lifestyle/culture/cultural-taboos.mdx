Then provide a categorized taboo guide covering the following areas. For each taboo, include what not to do, why it causes offense, the severity level rated as mildly awkward, noticeably rude, or deeply offensive, and what to do instead.

Gestures and body language. Cover hand signs, pointing methods, foot placement, head touching, eye contact norms, and physical contact between genders or across age groups. Flag any gestures that are positive in my home culture but offensive in the target culture.

Conversation and language. List topics that are off-limits or risky in casual conversation. Cover politics, religion, money, family size, personal appearance, and any historical events that remain sensitive. Explain how directness and disagreement are perceived.

Clothing and appearance. Describe dress code violations that cause genuine offense rather than mild surprise. Cover religious sites, government buildings, formal occasions, and everyday public spaces. Note gender-specific rules where they exist.

Dining and hospitality. Cover table manners that differ from my home culture, foods or drinks to refuse or accept carefully, tipping taboos, and guest behavior expectations. Include mistakes that insult a host.

Gift-giving. Explain which gifts carry negative symbolism. Cover colors, numbers, wrapping, and items to avoid. Note whether gifts should be opened immediately or set aside.

Religious and sacred spaces. List behaviors that violate religious norms at temples, mosques, churches, shrines, or other sacred sites. Cover photography, footwear, head covering, and conduct during prayers or ceremonies.

If I described a specific situation, provide a pre-event checklist tailored to that context. Walk me through the most likely taboos I could encounter from arrival to departure.

If I mentioned personal factors, flag any conflicts between my habits and local taboos. Suggest practical adjustments.

Close with a ranked list of the five most serious taboos to remember. For each one, give a single sentence I can review quickly before entering a social situation. If certain taboo violations carry legal consequences, mention them clearly.

Throughout this guide, note where taboos vary between urban and rural settings, between generations, and between regions within the country. Acknowledge that individual tolerance varies and that these patterns describe general expectations rather than absolute rules.
