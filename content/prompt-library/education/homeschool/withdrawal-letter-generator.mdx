# School Withdrawal Letter Generator

You are a homeschool parent's letter-writing partner for formally withdrawing a child from the school they currently attend. This letter goes to the current school or district to close out an existing enrollment. It is not the same document as a notice of intent to homeschool, which goes to a state or district to open a new one. A family moving a child mid-year needs both. A family starting a first child in kindergarten as a homeschooler needs only the notice of intent, since there is no prior enrollment to close.

Draft a withdrawal letter for [CHILD_NAME], currently enrolled at [CURRENT_SCHOOL_NAME], effective [WITHDRAWAL_DATE]. The letter is from [PARENT_NAME]. If you know the specific school district, name it in [SCHOOL_DISTRICT?]. If you want a reason stated, note it in [REASON?], otherwise the letter will state the withdrawal plainly without elaborating on the reason.

1. Write a formal header and opening line: [PARENT_NAME]'s contact information, the date, the recipient at [CURRENT_SCHOOL_NAME] or [SCHOOL_DISTRICT?], and a direct statement that [CHILD_NAME] is being withdrawn effective [WITHDRAWAL_DATE].

2. If [REASON?] was provided, state it in one plain sentence. If it was left blank, do not invent one, a withdrawal letter is valid without a stated reason.

3. Request the specific items a family typically needs from a school after withdrawal: the child's official records or transcript, any immunization records on file, and confirmation the withdrawal has been processed.

4. Close with a courteous but firm line confirming the withdrawal date is final, and a signature line for [PARENT_NAME].

Flag that some districts require withdrawal to be submitted on a specific form rather than a letter, or require it in person, and that submitting a notice of intent to homeschool is a separate step this letter does not cover, so I know to check both before treating the transition as complete.
