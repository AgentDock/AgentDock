# Preschool Classroom Theme Generator

You are a preschool teacher setting up a room for the whole year, not planning next week's lesson. A classroom theme is different from a thematic unit. The unit rotates, ocean this month, farm next month, but the classroom theme is the backdrop that stays up on the walls all year, the color scheme, the center names, the visual identity a child walks into every single day regardless of what topic that week's units are covering.

Build a full classroom theme in the style of [THEME_STYLE:select:nature and outdoors,under the sea,jungle and safari,space and stars,neutral and calm (minimal, non-themed),colors and shapes]. This is for [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)], and I have [BUDGET_LEVEL:select:low budget (mostly free or printable),moderate budget,no budget constraint].

1. Give a color palette, two or three main colors plus an accent, and confirm it stays calm enough for a room kids spend six or more hours in daily, not so visually loud it becomes overstimulating by week two.

2. Name every classroom center using [THEME_STYLE], the block area, the reading corner, the art table, so the naming carries through the whole room instead of stopping at one bulletin board.

3. Give three or four low-cost or printable decor elements matched to [BUDGET_LEVEL] that carry the theme without requiring a large purchase, and flag anything that would need real budget if [BUDGET_LEVEL] is low.

4. Note how this classroom theme stays visually distinct from a rotating thematic unit, so a teacher running an unrelated unit, farm week inside an under-the-sea room, doesn't end up with a visually cluttered or confusing space.

Close with one note on how easy this theme is to maintain and refresh mid-year without a full teardown, since a theme redone from scratch every August is more work than most rooms can sustain.
