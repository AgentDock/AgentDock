# Passport Checklist

I need a complete passport checklist so I can gather every required document and avoid delays or rejections during the application process.

My application type is [APPLICATION_TYPE:select:first-time adult passport,passport renewal,child passport under 16,lost or stolen replacement,name change update,damaged passport replacement].

My citizenship documentation is [CITIZENSHIP_DOCUMENT:select:U.S. birth certificate,naturalization certificate,certificate of citizenship,previous U.S. passport,consular report of birth abroad].

My current timeline is [TIMELINE:select:routine processing 6 to 8 weeks,expedited processing 2 to 3 weeks,urgent travel within 2 weeks,emergency life or death travel]. My travel date is [TRAVEL_DATE?].

I plan to apply at [APPLICATION_LOCATION:select:post office acceptance facility,county clerk office,public library passport office,federal passport agency,special passport acceptance event].

Special circumstances: [SPECIAL_CIRCUMSTANCES?] (such as applying for a minor without both parents present, name discrepancy on documents, previous passport denial, or dual citizenship considerations)

Generate a detailed passport checklist organized into clear action sections. Start with a document gathering section that lists every required form, proof of identity, and citizenship evidence for my specific application type. Include the correct form number (DS-11 for new applications or DS-82 for eligible renewals) and where to download or obtain it.

Add a passport photo section with current specifications for dimensions, background color, glasses rules, and acceptable expressions. Note common photo rejection reasons and tips for getting an approved photo on the first try.

Include a fees section with the current fee breakdown for my application type, accepted payment methods, and separate checks required for the application fee versus the execution fee. Flag which fee applies at which step.

Create a timeline section that maps out my selected processing speed, including realistic wait times, how to track application status, and when to consider upgrading to expedited service. If my timeline is urgent, include instructions for scheduling an appointment at a regional passport agency.

Add a common mistakes section covering the top reasons applications get delayed or rejected, such as unsigned forms, damaged birth certificates, mismatched names, and incorrect photo formats. For each mistake, provide the specific fix.

Finish with a day-of-appointment section listing everything to bring, what to expect at the acceptance facility, and steps to complete after submitting. Include reminders about keeping copies of all documents submitted.
