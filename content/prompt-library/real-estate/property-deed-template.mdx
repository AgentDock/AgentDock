I need to create a property deed to transfer ownership of real property. The property legal description or address is [PROPERTY_DESCRIPTION]. The consideration amount for this transfer is $[CONSIDERATION:number:0-100000000]. The vesting type for the grantee is [VESTING:select:sole ownership,joint tenants with right of survivorship,tenants in common,tenants by the entirety,community property]. Any existing encumbrances, easements, or restrictions that should be noted in the deed are [ENCUMBRANCES?].

Please generate a complete property deed document that includes a title block identifying the deed type and the recording information section with spaces for the county recorder. Begin with a preamble paragraph that states the effective date, identifies the grantor and grantee with their full names and addresses, and recites the consideration. Follow this with a granting clause that uses the legally appropriate conveyance language for the selected deed type, transferring all of the grantor's right, title, and interest in the property.

Include a full legal description section where the property description is presented with a reference to the parcel identification number and the county where the property is situated. Add a habendum clause that defines the extent of ownership being conveyed and specifies the vesting arrangement selected above.

For a general warranty deed, include the six traditional covenants: covenant of seisin, covenant of right to convey, covenant against encumbrances, covenant of quiet enjoyment, covenant of warranty, and covenant of further assurances. For a special warranty deed, limit these covenants to the period of the grantor's ownership. For a quitclaim deed, omit all covenants and warranties. For a bargain and sale deed, include only the implied covenant that the grantor holds title, and for a bargain and sale deed with covenants, add the covenant against grantor's acts.

Include a section noting any exceptions to title such as existing easements, restrictions of record, current taxes, and any additional encumbrances provided above. Add a notarization block with spaces for the notary public signature, commission expiration date, seal, and the state and county where the acknowledgment takes place. Include witness lines if required by the property's state. End with signature blocks for all grantors, including printed name lines and spaces for dates.
