# Employee of the Month Certificate

You are a senior HR communications specialist with 12 years of experience writing employee recognition documents, award programs, and formal certificates for organizations spanning technology, healthcare, finance, retail, and manufacturing. You have designed monthly, quarterly, and annual recognition programs for companies with 50 to 20,000 employees. You understand that the wording on an employee of the month certificate must strike the right balance between formal acknowledgment and genuine warmth, ensuring the recipient feels personally valued while the document remains professional enough to display or frame.

I need you to create a complete employee of the month certificate for the following recognition.

The company name is [COMPANY_NAME] and the certificate is being awarded to [EMPLOYEE_NAME], who holds the position of [JOB_TITLE] in the [DEPARTMENT] department. The award is for the month and year of [AWARD_MONTH_YEAR].

The award category is [AWARD_CATEGORY:select:Outstanding Performance,Leadership and Initiative,Customer Service Excellence,Innovation and Problem Solving,Team Collaboration,Sales Achievement,Safety Champion,Quality Excellence,Mentorship and Development,Going Above and Beyond].

The employee is being recognized for the following specific accomplishments or behaviors: [ACCOMPLISHMENTS]. Include measurable results, notable projects, or specific instances that set this employee apart during the award period.

The certificate is being presented by [PRESENTER_NAME], who holds the title of [PRESENTER_TITLE:select:CEO,President,Vice President,Director,Department Head,General Manager,HR Director,Team Lead,Regional Manager,Owner].

The tone for the certificate should be [TONE:select:Formal and Traditional,Professional but Warm,Celebratory and Enthusiastic,Simple and Elegant,Modern and Casual].

The certificate style is [CERTIFICATE_STYLE:select:Classic with Border Design,Minimalist Professional,Corporate Branded,Elegant with Gold Accents,Modern Clean Layout].

Additional context about the recognition program or special circumstances: [ADDITIONAL_CONTEXT?]. Examples include whether this is part of a peer-nominated program, the first time this award has been given, or if the employee overcame a significant challenge.

Generate a complete employee of the month certificate document with the following sections.

Certificate Header. The company name in a prominent position, followed by the formal certificate title. Include a decorative description note suggesting border style based on the [CERTIFICATE_STYLE] selection.

Formal Presentation Line. A one-line statement such as "This certificate is proudly presented to" followed by the employee's full name displayed prominently.

Award Designation. The full award title including the category and the month and year. Format as "Employee of the Month" followed by the [AWARD_CATEGORY] and [AWARD_MONTH_YEAR].

Recognition Statement. A two-to-three paragraph personalized body that opens with a congratulatory statement, describes the specific accomplishments from [ACCOMPLISHMENTS], explains why these contributions matter to the team and organization, and connects the achievement to the values or mission of [COMPANY_NAME]. Write in third person referring to the employee by name. Avoid generic phrases. Every sentence should tie back to what this specific person did during this specific period.

Achievement Highlights. A bulleted list of three to five key accomplishments pulled from the [ACCOMPLISHMENTS] details, each written as a concise, impactful statement with quantifiable results where available.

Quote or Commendation. A brief one-to-two sentence commendation written as if spoken by the [PRESENTER_NAME], expressing personal appreciation for the employee's contributions. Frame this as a direct quote.

Signature Block. Include lines for the [PRESENTER_NAME] with their [PRESENTER_TITLE], a line for the HR representative signature, and the date of presentation. Include printed name and title beneath each signature line.

Certificate Footer. The company name, a note about the recognition program, and a certificate reference number formatted as EOTM-YYYYMM-NNN.

Format the entire certificate using Markdown with clear section separators. Use emphasis and heading levels to create visual hierarchy that would translate well when transferred to a designed template. Match the overall language and formality to the selected [TONE] while keeping the content specific and personal to the recipient.
