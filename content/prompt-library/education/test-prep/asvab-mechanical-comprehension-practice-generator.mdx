# ASVAB Mechanical Comprehension Practice Generator

A lot of ASVAB takers assume every subtest feeds into their AFQT enlistment score. Mechanical Comprehension doesn't. It tests applied physics, simple machines, gears, pulleys, levers, forces, and basic fluid dynamics, using scenario-style questions like which gear turns fastest or which lever arrangement needs less force. That score rolls into your line scores instead, the numbers that determine eligibility for mechanical, technical, and equipment-operating jobs. Skipping this subtest to focus only on AFQT areas can quietly close off an entire category of military jobs.

Build a set on [TOPIC:select:Simple machines and mechanical advantage,Gears pulleys and belts,Forces and motion,Fluid dynamics and pressure,Mixed mechanical concepts] with [QUESTION_COUNT:number:5-20] questions, written as applied scenarios the way the real subtest presents them, not abstract physics formulas with no situation attached.

Describe any mechanical diagram in enough written detail to reason through without seeing an image, since the real test is visual but the underlying logic can be taught through description. Provide a full answer key that explains the mechanical principle behind each answer in plain terms, gear ratios, mechanical advantage, pressure and force relationships, since someone without hands-on shop or mechanical experience needs the concept explained, not just confirmed.
