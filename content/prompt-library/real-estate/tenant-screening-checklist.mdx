# Tenant Screening Checklist

You are an experienced property management consultant who has helped landlords evaluate thousands of prospective tenants. You understand that effective tenant screening balances thoroughness with Fair Housing compliance, and that the goal is to find reliable tenants who will pay rent on time, maintain the property, and fulfill their lease obligations.

I need a detailed tenant screening checklist for a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,apartment unit,condo,townhouse,duplex,multi-family building,commercial space] with a monthly rent of [MONTHLY_RENT]. The property is located in [PROPERTY_LOCATION] and I am screening tenants as a [LANDLORD_TYPE:select:individual landlord with one property,individual landlord with multiple properties,property management company,real estate investor,on-site manager for apartment complex].

My minimum acceptable credit score is [MIN_CREDIT_SCORE:number:300-850] and I require applicants to demonstrate a gross monthly income of at least [INCOME_RATIO:select:2x monthly rent,2.5x monthly rent,3x monthly rent,3.5x monthly rent,4x monthly rent] before approval. I want to verify rental history going back [HISTORY_PERIOD:select:1 year,2 years,3 years,5 years] and I [BACKGROUND_CHECK:select:require a full criminal background check,require a limited background check for felonies only,do not run criminal background checks,use a third-party screening service for all checks].

My current screening process is [CURRENT_PROCESS:select:informal with no standard procedure,partially documented but inconsistent,fully documented and consistently applied,brand new as I am screening tenants for the first time]. Any specific concerns or property rules I want to screen for include [SPECIFIC_CONCERNS?].

Please generate a complete tenant screening checklist that I can follow consistently for every applicant. Start with the application intake process, explaining what documents to collect upfront and what disclosures to provide before screening begins. Cover the credit check section in detail, including what to look for beyond the score itself such as payment patterns, outstanding collections, recent inquiries, and debt-to-income warning signs.

Include a thorough income and employment verification section that accounts for different employment types including salaried workers, self-employed applicants, retirees, and those with non-traditional income sources. Explain how to verify each type with appropriate documentation and what constitutes acceptable proof.

Address rental history verification with specific questions to ask previous landlords, how to spot fake references, and what patterns in rental history should raise concerns. Cover the background check process including what types of records to review, how to interpret results fairly, and what legal limitations apply to using criminal history in tenant decisions.

Include a section on personal and professional references, what to ask, and how to weigh reference responses alongside other screening data. Provide a Fair Housing compliance reminder covering protected classes, consistent screening criteria, and documentation practices that protect against discrimination claims. Explain how to apply screening criteria uniformly across all applicants and what records to keep.

End with a decision framework that helps me weigh all collected information, identify common red flags, determine when to approve, conditionally approve with additional deposit or cosigner, or deny an application. Include guidance on adverse action notice requirements when denying an applicant based on screening results. Note that landlord-tenant laws vary by jurisdiction and recommend consulting a local attorney or housing authority for state and municipal requirements that may impose additional obligations.
