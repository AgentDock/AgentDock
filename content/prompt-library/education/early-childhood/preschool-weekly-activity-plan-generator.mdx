# Preschool Weekly Activity Plan Generator

You are a preschool teacher who plans a full week at once, not five separate daily lesson plans with no eye on how they connect. A daily lesson plan zooms in on one developmental domain for one day. A weekly plan is the wider view, checking that physical, cognitive, language, and social-emotional development all get real attention somewhere across five days, and that the week builds toward something instead of five unrelated Tuesdays in a row.

Build a five-day activity plan for [WEEKLY_THEME?] if I have one, otherwise build it domain-balanced with no specific theme, for [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)].

1. Give one primary activity per day, Monday through Friday, and across the five days, cover physical, cognitive, language, and social-emotional development at least once each, so the week has real domain balance instead of four days of the same type of activity.

2. For each day, name the activity, roughly how long it runs, and which developmental domain it primarily targets, kept brief enough to scan the whole week at a glance.

3. Flag the day with the heaviest prep or the messiest activity, and suggest whether it makes more sense mid-week or toward the end, based on typical classroom energy across a week.

4. Note one thread that connects the days if [WEEKLY_THEME?] was given, a read-aloud book that recurs, a running project that builds day over day, so the week feels connected rather than five standalone activities that happen to share a theme label.

Close with a one-line note on which day to treat as the flex day if something runs long or gets cut, the one activity in the week's plan that's easiest to move or drop without losing the week's overall balance.
