# Destination Wedding Checklist

I need a complete destination wedding checklist so I can plan every detail of getting married abroad without missing critical steps.

My wedding destination is [DESTINATION] and our planned wedding date is [WEDDING_DATE].

We expect approximately [GUEST_COUNT:select:under 20 guests,20 to 50 guests,50 to 100 guests,over 100 guests] to attend.

Our overall wedding budget is [BUDGET_RANGE:select:under $10000,between $10000 and $25000,between $25000 and $50000,over $50000].

The wedding style we want is [WEDDING_STYLE:select:beach ceremony,garden or vineyard,historic venue or castle,resort all-inclusive,city elopement,religious or cultural ceremony].

We are citizens of [HOME_COUNTRY] and need to understand the legal requirements for marrying in our destination.

Any special considerations: [SPECIAL_CONSIDERATIONS?] (such as religious customs, cultural traditions, accessibility needs, multi-day celebrations, or dietary restrictions for the group)

Generate a thorough destination wedding checklist organized by timeline milestones. Start with 12 months before the wedding, covering destination research, venue scouting, and early legal research for marrying abroad. Include requirements like marriage license rules, residency periods, document translations, and apostille needs for the destination country. Note that marriage laws vary by country and change frequently, so recommend verifying all legal requirements directly with the destination country's consulate or a local wedding planner.

Move to 9 months out with vendor coordination across distance, including how to evaluate local photographers, caterers, florists, and officiants when you cannot visit in person. Cover contract considerations for international vendors, currency and payment logistics, and backup vendor identification.

At 6 months, cover guest travel coordination including save-the-dates with travel details, hotel room blocks, group flight options, and a wedding website with destination guides, local transportation tips, and weather expectations. Include guidance on welcome bags with local treats and printed itineraries for guests.

At 3 months, address attire logistics for transporting or shipping wedding clothes internationally, final vendor confirmations, group activity planning for the days surrounding the wedding, rehearsal dinner arrangements, and marriage license application timelines.

At 1 month, cover final guest headcounts, seating arrangements, day-of timeline creation, emergency kit preparation, and contingency plans for weather disruptions or vendor cancellations. Include tips for coordinating across time zones when finalizing details with local vendors.

In the final week, list arrival logistics, vendor walk-throughs, marriage license pickup, welcome event hosting, and a detailed day-of schedule. After the wedding, include guidance on legal document filing, name change processes across countries, vendor tip distribution, and thank-you note organization.

For each checklist item, note who is responsible (couple, planner, family member, or vendor) and flag items that vary by destination country. Adapt the budget recommendations and vendor guidance to match our [BUDGET_RANGE] and [GUEST_COUNT]. Include destination-specific notes for [DESTINATION] when possible.
