# Possessive Pronoun and Adjective Practice Generator

English picks its possessive based on who owns something, his book, her book, no matter what the book is. Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, and Russian all flip that logic. The possessive agrees with the thing being owned, not the owner, so a French speaker's son livre can mean his book or her book depending entirely on context, since son just means the book is masculine singular, and says nothing about who owns it. An English speaker who doesn't unlearn the English logic first keeps reaching for a possessive based on the wrong noun.

Include at least a few items where the owner's gender differs from what an English speaker might assume drives the possessive, so the agrees-with-the-object rule actually gets tested rather than accidentally matching by coincidence.

If Spanish, French, or German was selected, note that the possessive replaces the article rather than joining it, mi libro and mon livre and mein Buch, not a separate article stacked in front.
