# Cultural Norms

I am from one culture and need a practical guide to the cultural norms of another so I can interact respectfully and avoid accidental offense.

My home culture or country is [HOME_CULTURE], and the culture I want to understand is [TARGET_CULTURE].

My primary reason for learning is [CONTEXT:select:traveling as a tourist,relocating for work,starting a cross-cultural relationship,studying abroad,hosting visitors from this culture,doing business internationally,attending a cultural event or ceremony,general curiosity].

The areas of daily life I care about most are [FOCUS_AREAS] (for example, greetings and introductions, personal space and physical contact, gift-giving, punctuality, conversation topics, dress codes, religious observances, or workplace behavior).

My familiarity with the target culture so far is [FAMILIARITY:select:no prior knowledge,some exposure through media or friends,visited once or twice,lived there briefly,moderate familiarity but want deeper understanding].

The level of detail I want is [DETAIL_LEVEL:select:a quick overview of the top rules,a thorough guide organized by situation,a deep reference covering history and reasoning behind each norm].

If there is a specific upcoming situation I need to prepare for: [SITUATION?] (such as a first business meeting, dinner at a local home, a wedding, a religious holiday, or meeting a partner's family)

Any personal constraints I should mention: [CONSTRAINTS?] (such as dietary restrictions, mobility needs, religious practices of my own, or topics I am sensitive about)

Start by giving me a brief orientation to the target culture. Explain the core values that shape everyday behavior, what the society prioritizes, and how those priorities differ from my home culture. Keep this section short and focused on the values that directly affect social interaction.

Then provide a detailed norms guide organized by situation. Cover greetings and first impressions, including what to say, whether to bow or shake hands, and how formality changes depending on age or status. Cover conversation norms, including safe topics, topics to avoid entirely, the expected volume and pace of speech, and how directness or indirectness is perceived. Cover personal space, eye contact, and physical contact rules, noting any differences between genders or generations. Cover punctuality expectations for social, professional, and formal occasions.

If I selected specific focus areas, give those the most detailed treatment. For each norm you describe, explain the reasoning behind it so I understand the cultural logic rather than just memorizing a list of rules. Include what the norm looks like in practice, what a common mistake looks like, and what the local reaction would be if someone broke the norm.

Add a section on do's and don'ts organized as two clear lists. The do's should cover positive behaviors that locals appreciate from visitors. The don'ts should cover the mistakes most likely to cause genuine offense rather than minor awkwardness. Rank the don'ts from most serious to least serious.

If I described a specific situation, provide a step-by-step walkthrough of how to behave from arrival to departure. Cover what to wear, what to bring, how to greet people, what to expect during the event, and how to leave gracefully.

Include a section on how these norms vary within the target culture. Norms often differ between urban and rural areas, between generations, and between regions. Highlight the most significant variations so I do not over-generalize.

Close with a quick-reference card listing the five most important norms to remember ranked by how much they matter. For each one, give me a single sentence I can review right before entering a social situation. If there are key phrases or words I should learn in the local language, list them with pronunciation guidance.

Throughout the guide, acknowledge that individuals vary and cultural norms describe general patterns rather than rigid rules that every person follows. Present the information as practical guidance for building respectful first impressions while staying open to each person's individuality.
