# Travel Journal Template

I want a structured travel journal template that helps me capture the details of each day so I can look back on this trip with vivid, specific memories instead of vague impressions.

I am currently in [DESTINATION] on day [DAY_NUMBER] of my trip (today's date included in the entry).

The weather today was [WEATHER:select:sunny and warm,partly cloudy,overcast,rainy,cold and clear,hot and humid,windy,snowy,perfect].

My overall mood today is [MOOD:select:excited and energized,relaxed and content,tired but happy,overwhelmed in a good way,reflective and thoughtful,adventurous,homesick but grateful,inspired].

The highlight of today was [HIGHLIGHT] (the single best moment, experience, or discovery of the day).

Places I visited today: [PLACES_VISITED] (list the specific neighborhoods, landmarks, parks, museums, markets, or other locations).

Food and drink worth remembering: [FOOD_NOTES] (describe what you ate, where you ate it, what made it memorable, and whether you would recommend it).

People I met or interacted with: [PEOPLE_MET?] (locals, fellow travelers, guides, vendors, or anyone whose conversation or kindness stood out).

Surprises, sensory details, or lessons from today: [REFLECTIONS?] (unexpected discoveries, specific sounds or smells that defined the day, cultural insights, or anything you learned about the place or yourself).

How much I spent today and overall rating: [DAY_SUMMARY?] (rough spending total, rate the day 1-10, and note whether you would recommend this route to others).

Using all of this information, write a vivid and personal journal entry for this day of my trip. Write in first person. Open with the setting and mood of the morning. Move through the day chronologically, weaving in the sensory details, food notes, and people encounters at the points where they happened naturally. Include honest reflections, not just positive highlights. Close with an evening reflection that connects what I experienced today to what I am looking forward to tomorrow. Keep the tone genuine and conversational, as if writing for my future self to read years from now. Add a short "Quick Reference" box at the end with the day's top recommendation, a food pick, an estimated daily cost, and one practical tip for anyone visiting the same places.
