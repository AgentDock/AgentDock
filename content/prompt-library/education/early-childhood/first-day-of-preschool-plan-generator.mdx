# First Day of Preschool Plan Generator

You are a preschool director planning the actual operational first day, not a list of icebreaker games. The first day of preschool is dominated by two things an icebreaker doesn't touch: getting the physical room and routines right when nobody knows them yet, and managing separation anxiety for kids who may never have been away from a parent for this long before. A strong first day plan is closer to an event run sheet than a lesson plan.

Build a first day plan for a class of [CLASS_SIZE:select:small (under 10),medium (10 to 15),large (15 plus)] at [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)]. Set [STAFFING:select:lead teacher only,lead teacher plus one aide,lead teacher plus multiple aides] to match my actual staffing.

1. Give a simple hour-by-hour schedule for the first day, shorter and simpler than a typical day, with more transition buffer built in than usual since everything will run slower while kids and staff learn the routine together.

2. Give a specific separation anxiety strategy, what a teacher says to a distressed child, what a teacher tells a lingering parent, and a signal for when a parent should actually leave rather than prolonging the goodbye, since a long goodbye typically makes separation harder, not easier.

3. Give a room setup checklist specific to day one, name labels visible and legible, cubbies clearly marked, a designated calm-down or quiet spot ready before a single child arrives, matched to [CLASS_SIZE] and [STAFFING].

4. Give one thing each staff member should be watching for across the room in the first hour specifically, matched to [STAFFING], so coverage is intentional rather than everyone hovering near the door at drop-off.

Close with one note on what a realistic first day actually looks like emotionally, some tears, some kids acclimating instantly, so staff go in with calibrated expectations instead of treating a hard morning as a sign something went wrong.
