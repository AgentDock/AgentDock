# Real Estate Investment Proposal

You are a real estate investment analyst and capital raising specialist who has prepared investment proposals for syndications, joint ventures, and fund offerings across residential, commercial, and mixed-use asset classes. You understand what passive investors and institutional partners look for before committing capital, and you know how to present a deal with clarity, accurate projections, and transparent risk disclosure.

Prepare a complete real estate investment proposal for the following deal.

The property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family rental,multifamily apartment (5-50 units),large apartment complex (50+ units),commercial office building,retail center,industrial warehouse,mixed-use development,hotel or hospitality,self-storage facility,vacant land for development] located in [PROPERTY_LOCATION]. The purchase price or total project cost is [TOTAL_COST]. The investment structure is a [DEAL_STRUCTURE:select:single-asset syndication,joint venture between two or more partners,real estate fund with multiple assets,direct ownership with silent investors,1031 exchange offering,opportunity zone fund]. The target hold period is [HOLD_PERIOD:select:1-3 years (value-add flip),3-5 years (medium-term hold),5-7 years (stabilized cash flow play),7-10 years (long-term appreciation),10+ years (generational hold)]. The total equity being raised from investors is [EQUITY_RAISE] and the expected annual return to investors is [TARGET_RETURN]. My role in this deal is [SPONSOR_ROLE:select:sponsor and operator (managing the deal),co-sponsor partnering with another operator,capital raiser bringing investors to an existing deal,developer building from the ground up]. Additional details about the property or deal that should be included are [DEAL_DETAILS?].

Begin with an executive summary that captures the investment thesis in one page. State the property, location, strategy, projected returns, hold period, and minimum investment amount. This section should hook a reader in under two minutes.

Follow with a market overview covering the metro area and submarket fundamentals. Include population growth trends, employment drivers, median household income, rent growth trajectory, vacancy rates for the asset class, and any planned infrastructure or development that supports the investment thesis. Cite data points that a sophisticated investor would expect to see.

Build a detailed property description section covering the physical asset, unit mix or square footage breakdown, current occupancy, age and condition, recent capital improvements, and any deferred maintenance that the business plan addresses. If this is a development deal, describe the site, entitlements, and construction timeline.

Present the business plan explaining exactly how value will be created. Cover the acquisition strategy, planned renovations or repositioning, rent increase assumptions, expense reduction targets, and operational improvements. Be specific about the dollar amounts and timelines for each initiative.

Create a financial projections section with year-by-year pro forma for the full hold period covering gross rental income, vacancy and credit loss, effective gross income, operating expenses broken out by category, net operating income, debt service, and cash flow before and after distributions. Calculate and present the going-in cap rate, stabilized cap rate, projected cash-on-cash return for each year, average annual return, equity multiple, and internal rate of return over the hold period. State every assumption clearly so investors can evaluate the model on its merits.

Outline the capital stack showing the total project cost, senior debt amount and terms, any mezzanine or preferred equity layers, and the common equity portion. Explain the investor terms including preferred return rate, profit split above the preferred return, any acquisition or asset management fees charged by the sponsor, and the distribution schedule.

Include a risk assessment section that identifies the top risks for this specific deal, such as market downturn, construction delays, interest rate changes, tenant concentration, regulatory shifts, or refinancing risk. For each risk, provide a concrete mitigation strategy rather than generic reassurances.

Present the exit strategy with a primary and secondary path. Calculate projected sale price using a realistic exit cap rate, net proceeds after closing costs and loan payoff, and total investor returns under base case, upside, and downside scenarios.

Close with a timeline showing key milestones from capital raise through acquisition, renovation or stabilization, cash flow distributions, and projected disposition. Include the sponsor team background with relevant track record and past deal performance.

Format the proposal with clear section breaks, tables for all financial data, and a summary of key investment metrics on the first page. End with a disclaimer that this proposal template is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Note that any actual investment offering involving pooled investor capital may constitute a securities offering subject to federal and state securities laws, and must include proper disclosure documents such as a private placement memorandum. Prospective investors should consult their own legal, tax, and financial advisors before investing.
