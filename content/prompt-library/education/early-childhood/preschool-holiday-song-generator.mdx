# Preschool Holiday Song Generator

You are a preschool teacher who knows a song without a motion loses half a 3 year old's attention span by the second verse. Preschool music sticks because it's physical, clapping, stomping, finger movements that mirror the words, not because the melody or lyrics alone are memorable. Writing a new seasonal song means writing the movement at the same time as the words, not adding it as an afterthought.

Write a [HOLIDAY_SEASON:select:fall,halloween,thanksgiving,christmas,winter (general),spring,valentine's day,st. patrick's day] song for [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)]. Set [FORMAT:select:new lyrics to a familiar tune,original chant with no tune] to choose the format. If you're writing to a familiar tune, use [TUNE?] if I named one, otherwise pick a tune most preschool classrooms already know, "Twinkle Twinkle," "The Wheels on the Bus," "Row Row Row Your Boat," and name which one you used.

1. Write the full lyrics, kept to two or three short verses so [AGE_GROUP] can hold the whole thing in memory within a week of repetition, and matched to a vocabulary level that age can actually say out loud without stumbling.

2. Write the hand motions or fingerplay for every line, specific enough that a teacher could demonstrate it once and have kids follow along, not a vague "act it out" note.

3. If [FORMAT] is new lyrics to a tune, confirm the syllable count and rhythm actually fits [TUNE?] or the tune you picked, line by line, so it doesn't just read as a poem that happens to be near the right length.

4. Note whether this works better standing in a circle, seated with just hand motions, or moving around the room, so a teacher knows how to set up the space before starting.

Close with one line on which part of the song kids usually latch onto first, the repeated chorus line, a specific silly motion, so a teacher knows where to put the energy on day one.
