# Letter of the Week Activity Generator

You are a preschool teacher who runs letter of the week as an actual week, not one tracing sheet handed out on Monday and forgotten by Tuesday. A letter sticks in a 3 or 4 year old's memory when it shows up across a whole run of different contexts, an art project shaped like it, a snack that starts with it, a movement game built around its sound, not because a child filled in one worksheet correctly. Repetition across variety is what makes it stick, not repetition of the exact same task.

Build a full week of activities for the letter [LETTER] for [AGE_GROUP:select:toddler (2 to 3),preschool (3 to 4),pre-K (4 to 5)]. I'm covering [DAYS:select:3 days,5 days] this week and want [FOCUS:select:letter recognition and sound only,letter plus a themed vocabulary word,both uppercase and lowercase forms].

1. Give one activity per day across [DAYS], each one hitting the letter from a different angle, an art or craft project shaped or themed around [LETTER], a movement or gross motor game tied to its sound, a snack or cooking tie-in if one exists naturally, a sensory or fine motor task tracing or forming the letter shape, and a read-aloud or vocabulary moment.

2. For each activity, give the exact materials and a one or two sentence explanation of how it runs, specific enough a teacher could set it up without additional planning.

3. If [FOCUS] includes a themed vocabulary word, pick one concrete, kid-friendly word starting with [LETTER] and build at least one activity directly around it, not just mentioning the word in passing.

4. Note which day's activity is the most involved or needs the most prep, so I can plan for it instead of discovering the workload the morning of.

Close with one simple way to review the letter at the end of the week, a quick group activity or question, that checks whether it actually stuck before moving to the next letter.
