# Spanish Reflexive Pronoun Practice Generator

A learner wants to say they fell asleep during the movie and writes dormí durante la película, which just means they slept during the movie, maybe the whole thing, maybe on purpose, with no sense of an unplanned moment of drifting off. Dormirse is the version that means fell asleep, the reflexive pronoun marking the shift from a general state to a specific event happening to the subject. Losing that pronoun doesn't just weaken the sentence, it changes what happened in it. This generator drills exactly the verbs where the reflexive and non-reflexive versions mean different things.

State the actual meaning shift plainly for each pair instead of leaving it implied. Ir means to go, heading toward a place, while irse means to leave, the emphasis on departing from wherever the subject currently is, voy a la tienda against me voy right now, from here. Dormir names sleep as a state, dormirse names the moment of falling into it, dormí bien describes sleep quality while me dormí en el sofá describes a specific unplanned event. Poner means to physically put something somewhere, and ponerse splits into two distinct meanings depending on what follows it, putting on clothing, me pongo la chaqueta, or becoming an emotion or state, se puso triste.
