# Property Tax Appeal Letter

I need help writing a formal property tax appeal letter to my county assessor's office. My property is located at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]. The property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,condominium,townhouse,duplex,multi-family,manufactured home,vacant land] with approximately [SQUARE_FEET:number:500-15000] square feet of living space, built in [YEAR_BUILT:number:1900-2099]. The county's current assessed value is [ASSESSED_VALUE] but I believe the fair market value is closer to [ESTIMATED_FAIR_VALUE]. The appeal deadline for my jurisdiction is [APPEAL_DEADLINE].

The primary reasons I am contesting the assessment include [APPEAL_REASONS:select:comparable sales show lower values,property condition issues reduce value,assessment contains factual errors,recent market decline in my area,combination of multiple factors]. Specific conditions or deficiencies affecting the property's value include [PROPERTY_ISSUES?]. I have identified comparable properties that recently sold for less than my assessed value, and these comps include [COMPARABLE_SALES_DETAILS?].

Please draft a professional appeal letter addressed to the assessor that opens with a clear statement of my intent to formally protest the assessed value and references the relevant tax year and parcel number if available. The letter should present my comparable sales evidence in a structured way, explaining why each comp supports a lower valuation by noting similarities in location, size, age, and condition to my property. Where my comps differ from my property, include appropriate adjustments that a reasonable appraiser would apply.

If I indicated property condition issues, incorporate those into the argument by explaining how deferred maintenance, structural concerns, or functional obsolescence reduce the property's market value below the assessed figure. If there are factual errors in the assessment record such as incorrect square footage, wrong bedroom or bathroom count, or misclassified property type, present those corrections with a request to update the records.

Include a section that references the legal standard for assessment in most jurisdictions, which is that assessed value should reflect fair market value as of a specific valuation date. Note that the burden typically falls on the homeowner to demonstrate the assessment is incorrect, and frame the evidence accordingly. Mention that I am prepared to present this evidence at a formal hearing if the informal review does not resolve the dispute.

Close the letter with a professional request for a written response, a summary of the adjusted value I am requesting, and a note that supporting documentation such as photographs, repair estimates, and MLS listings for comparable sales are attached or available upon request. Add a disclaimer that this letter is prepared for informational purposes and that property tax laws vary by jurisdiction, so the homeowner should verify local appeal procedures, deadlines, and legal requirements with their county assessor's office or a qualified tax professional.
