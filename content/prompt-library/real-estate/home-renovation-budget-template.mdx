# Home Renovation Budget Template

I am planning a home renovation and need a complete budget template to track every cost before, during, and after the project. The renovation scope is [RENOVATION_SCOPE:select:single room remodel,kitchen renovation,bathroom renovation,multiple rooms,whole house renovation,addition or extension,basement finishing,exterior only]. My total budget is approximately [TOTAL_BUDGET:number:1000-500000] dollars. The home is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,condominium,duplex,mobile home,historic home] built in [YEAR_BUILT:number:1900-2026], and the renovation area covers roughly [AREA_SQFT:number:50-5000] square feet.

I plan to handle the work through [WORK_APPROACH:select:hiring a general contractor,managing subcontractors myself,doing most work as DIY,a mix of DIY and hired labor]. My timeline goal is [TIMELINE:select:under 1 month,1 to 3 months,3 to 6 months,6 to 12 months,over 12 months]. My priority is [PRIORITY:select:staying strictly within budget,getting the highest quality finishes,finishing as quickly as possible,maximizing resale value,balancing cost and quality].

Generate a detailed renovation budget template organized into the following sections. Start with a project summary that shows the total budget, contingency reserve amount, and a breakdown by major category. Use a contingency reserve of [CONTINGENCY:select:5%,10%,15%,20%] of the total budget for unexpected costs.

Create room-by-room or area-by-area cost breakdowns that match my renovation scope. For each area, list every anticipated expense grouped into demolition and removal, materials and supplies, fixtures and finishes, labor and installation, and permits or inspections. Include line items with estimated cost ranges based on the quality level my priority suggests.

Add a contractor and vendor tracking section with columns for trade or vendor name, contact info, bid amount, deposit paid, balance due, and payment schedule. Include space for at least [NUM_CONTRACTORS:number:2-15] contractors or vendors.

Include a timeline tracker that maps each phase of work to its budget allocation and expected start and completion dates. Flag items that must happen in sequence versus tasks that can overlap.

Finish with a spending tracker format that lets me log actual costs against estimates as the project progresses, calculate variance for each line item, and see a running total of remaining budget at any point during the renovation.
