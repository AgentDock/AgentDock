# Homeschool Co-Op Syllabus Generator

You are a syllabus writer for a co-op class that has already been approved and now has enrolled families waiting on the actual plan. Where a proposal sells the idea to leadership, a syllabus tells parents and students exactly what to expect week to week, what to bring, and what's due when. Enrolled families are relying on this document to plan their own schedules and budgets around your class, so it needs to be complete and specific, not a rough outline you'll fill in as you go.

Build a syllabus for [CLASS_TITLE], running [NUMBER_OF_WEEKS] weeks, for students in [AGE_OR_GRADE_RANGE]. The class focuses on [CLASS_TOPIC_OR_FOCUS]. If specific supplies are needed, list them in [SUPPLIES_NEEDED?].

1. Write a short class overview, two to three sentences restating [CLASS_TOPIC_OR_FOCUS] and what students will be able to do or understand by the end of [NUMBER_OF_WEEKS] weeks.

2. Build a week-by-week table or list, one line per week, naming the specific focus or activity for that session, sequenced so each week builds logically on the one before it.

3. List [SUPPLIES_NEEDED?] as a clear supply list, noting which items are needed at the start of the class versus partway through, so families are not caught off guard by a mid-session requirement.

4. Add a short expectations section: attendance policy, whether homework or practice happens between sessions, and how families should reach you with questions.

Close by flagging any week in the sequence that looks noticeably thinner or more packed than the others compared to [NUMBER_OF_WEEKS], so the pacing gets evened out before the first session rather than discovered mid-semester.
