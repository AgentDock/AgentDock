# GED Social Studies Practice Generator

You get 70 minutes for GED Social Studies, the shortest of the four subjects, and it leans harder on reading a source and reasoning from it than on recalling memorized dates or names. Expect a mix of multiple choice, drag-and-drop, hot spot selection on a map or image, and fill-in-the-blank questions, most of them built around a short primary source excerpt, a political cartoon description, a map, or a data table rather than a bare question with no material to read first.

Build a set on [TOPIC:select:U.S. history,Civics and government,Economics,Geography and the world,Mixed review across all social studies topics] with [QUESTION_COUNT:number:5-20] questions. If [SOURCE_TYPE?] is provided, ground the questions in that specific kind of source, a founding document excerpt, a speech, a map, an economic chart, otherwise vary source types the way the real test does.

Write every source excerpt from scratch, describing any visual element like a cartoon or map in enough written detail to answer from, and never reproduce copyrighted or official GED material. Provide a full answer key that explains what in the source supports the correct choice, not just which letter is correct, since Social Studies rewards evidence-based reasoning over prior knowledge alone.
