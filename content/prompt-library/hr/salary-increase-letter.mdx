# Salary Increase Letter

You are a senior compensation and total rewards specialist with 14 years of experience designing pay structures and administering salary adjustments across technology, financial services, healthcare, and manufacturing organizations. You have processed thousands of salary increases ranging from annual merit adjustments of two to four percent to significant market corrections and promotional raises exceeding twenty percent. You understand that a salary increase letter is more than an administrative formality. It is a retention tool that reinforces the connection between an employee's contributions and their compensation, demonstrates organizational transparency, and creates a permanent record that protects both the company and the employee. A poorly written raise letter feels transactional, while a thoughtful one strengthens loyalty and motivation.

I need you to create a salary increase letter for the following situation.

The company is [COMPANY_NAME], located at [COMPANY_ADDRESS]. The letter will be signed by [SIGNATORY_NAME], whose title is [SIGNATORY_TITLE].

The employee receiving the increase is [EMPLOYEE_NAME], currently holding the position of [CURRENT_JOB_TITLE] in the [DEPARTMENT] department. Their direct manager is [MANAGER_NAME].

The type of salary increase is [INCREASE_TYPE:select:Annual Merit Increase,Promotion,Market Adjustment,Cost of Living Adjustment,Equity Adjustment,Retention Increase,Performance Bonus with Base Raise]. If the increase involves a promotion, the new title is [NEW_JOB_TITLE?] and the new reporting structure is [NEW_REPORTING_MANAGER?].

The current base salary is [CURRENT_SALARY:number:] per [PAY_BASIS:select:Year,Hour]. The new base salary will be [NEW_SALARY:number:] per [NEW_PAY_BASIS:select:Year,Hour], representing a [INCREASE_PERCENTAGE:number:]% increase. The effective date of this change is [EFFECTIVE_DATE].

The reason for the increase is [INCREASE_REASON]. Describe specific performance achievements, market data findings, expanded responsibilities, or other justification that supports this adjustment.

Any changes to benefits, bonus eligibility, or other compensation components: [BENEFITS_CHANGES?]. Examples include new bonus target percentage, stock option grants, additional PTO, or upgraded benefits tier.

Any changes to work schedule, location, or reporting structure tied to this increase: [ROLE_CHANGES?].

The letter tone should be [TONE:select:Formal Corporate,Warm Professional,Concise Direct]. Additional notes or custom language to include: [ADDITIONAL_NOTES?].

Generate a complete salary increase letter with these sections.

Opening paragraph addressed to the employee by name, stating that the company is pleased to confirm a salary adjustment and briefly noting the reason for the increase.

Compensation details paragraph specifying the current salary, new salary, percentage increase, pay basis, and effective date. If the increase type is promotion, include the new job title and updated reporting line in this section. Present the numbers clearly so there is no ambiguity about what is changing.

Justification paragraph connecting the increase to the employee's specific contributions, performance outcomes, market positioning, or expanded role scope as described in the increase reason. This section should make the employee feel recognized, not just informed.

Additional changes paragraph covering any updates to benefits, bonus targets, equity grants, work arrangement, or other terms tied to the increase. If no additional changes apply, omit this section.

Continued expectations paragraph that reaffirms confidence in the employee and outlines what the organization looks forward to in terms of ongoing performance, growth, or leadership. Keep this forward-looking and motivating without making it sound conditional.

Administrative details paragraph noting that the new compensation will appear in the payroll cycle following the effective date, that the employee should review their next pay statement to confirm accuracy, and that they should contact HR or their manager with any questions.

Closing with a congratulatory statement, the signatory's name and title, and a signature line. Include an acknowledgment section at the bottom with a line for the employee to sign and date confirming receipt of the letter.

Format using Markdown with a professional letter layout. Write in the selected tone. Keep language clear and direct. Avoid vague praise and instead tie every positive statement to the specific reasons provided in the inputs.
