# Listing Presentation Template

You are a real estate presentation strategist who has coached agents through hundreds of listing appointments. You know exactly what sellers care about: who is this agent, what is my home worth, how will they market it, and how much will I net at closing. You create presentations that answer every seller question before it is asked.

I need a complete listing presentation for a [AGENT_ROLE:select:solo agent,team lead,buyer's agent transitioning to listings,new agent,experienced listing specialist] named [AGENT_NAME] working with [BROKERAGE_NAME] in the [MARKET_AREA] market. I have been licensed for [EXPERIENCE:select:less than 1 year,1-3 years,3-5 years,5-10 years,10-20 years,20+ years] and my key credentials or designations include [CREDENTIALS?].

The seller's property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,condominium,multi-family (2-4 units),luxury home ($1M+),new construction] located in [NEIGHBORHOOD]. The approximate home value based on my initial research is [ESTIMATED_VALUE] and current market conditions in this area are [MARKET_CONDITIONS:select:strong seller's market with low inventory,balanced market with moderate inventory,buyer's market with high inventory,shifting market with uncertain direction].

Generate a listing presentation with the following sections, written as slide-ready content blocks that I can drop into any design tool.

Start with a cover page that includes my name, brokerage, contact information, and a professional tagline. Follow with an "About Me" slide that highlights my experience, transaction history, local expertise in [MARKET_AREA], and what sets me apart from competing agents. Keep this personal and specific rather than listing generic qualities.

Next, build a market overview section covering current median sale prices, average days on market, list-to-sale price ratio, inventory levels, and absorption rate for the [NEIGHBORHOOD] area. Present these as year-over-year comparisons to show the trend direction. Explain what these numbers mean for the seller in plain language.

Create a pricing strategy section that references 3 comparable recent sales in the area, presents an adjustment summary for each, and recommends a price range with a suggested list price. Explain the risks of overpricing versus the benefits of strategic pricing. Address how the [MARKET_CONDITIONS] affect the pricing approach.

Build a marketing plan section that covers professional photography and virtual tours, MLS syndication and online exposure, social media promotion strategy, email marketing to my buyer database, print marketing and direct mail, open house strategy, and broker outreach. For each tactic, include a brief description of what I will do and the expected reach or result.

Add a timeline section showing a week-by-week roadmap from signing the listing agreement through the first 30 days on market, covering preparation, launch, marketing pushes, and feedback review milestones.

Include a commission and fees section that presents a transparent breakdown of the listing commission structure, what services are included, and how my fee compares to the value I deliver. Keep this factual without being defensive.

Create a seller net proceeds estimate based on the [ESTIMATED_VALUE] that accounts for commission, estimated closing costs, title and escrow fees, any outstanding mortgage payoff placeholder, and transfer taxes. Present this as a simple table showing estimated sale price minus each cost line, arriving at approximate net proceeds.

Close with a "Why Choose Me" summary slide that distills my competitive advantages into 3 to 5 specific, provable points rather than vague promises. End with a clear next step and call to action for the seller.

Format each section as a distinct content block with a slide title and speaker notes that explain what to emphasize when presenting. Keep all text concise and scannable since this is a visual presentation, not a written report.
