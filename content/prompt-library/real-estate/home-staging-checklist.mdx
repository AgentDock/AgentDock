# Home Staging Checklist

You are a certified home staging consultant with over a decade of experience helping sellers prepare properties that attract strong offers within the first two weeks on the market. I need a comprehensive, room-by-room staging checklist for my upcoming listing.

The property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,condominium,luxury estate,multi-family,vacation rental,new construction] located in [LOCATION] with [BEDROOMS:number:1-10] bedrooms and [BATHROOMS:number:1-8] bathrooms, totaling approximately [SQUARE_FEET:number:400-15000] square feet. The home was built in [YEAR_BUILT:number:1900-2099] and its current condition is [CONDITION:select:move-in ready with recent updates,good with minor cosmetic wear,fair with some dated finishes,needs significant refreshing].

My staging approach is [APPROACH:select:full DIY on a tight budget,DIY with selective professional rentals,professional stager for key rooms only,full professional staging throughout]. My total staging budget is approximately [BUDGET:select:under $500,between $500 and $1500,between $1500 and $5000,over $5000]. The target buyer for this market is [TARGET_BUYER:select:first-time home buyers,young families with children,move-up buyers seeking upgrades,downsizers and empty nesters,luxury buyers,investors and flippers]. I want the staged look to follow a [STYLE:select:modern minimalist,farmhouse warm,coastal relaxed,traditional classic,Scandinavian clean,mid-century modern,transitional neutral] aesthetic.

The rooms I need staged include [ROOMS:select:all rooms including garage and outdoor spaces,living room and kitchen and primary bedroom only,main living areas plus all bedrooms,common areas only for a condo or townhouse]. The listing goes live in [TIMELINE:select:one week,two weeks,three weeks,one month or more], and any special circumstances or features include [SPECIAL_NOTES?].

Please create a detailed staging checklist that starts with a whole-house preparation phase covering decluttering benchmarks, depersonalizing guidelines, deep cleaning standards, and any quick repairs or paint touch-ups that deliver the highest return for the cost. Then provide room-by-room staging instructions for each space included in my selected rooms, covering furniture arrangement, lighting, accessories, color palette, and sensory details like scent and temperature.

Include a curb appeal section addressing landscaping, front entry presentation, exterior paint or power washing needs, and outdoor living areas. Add a photography and showing readiness section that explains how to prepare the home for listing photos and how to maintain the staged look through the showing period.

For each task, note the estimated cost range and time required so I can plan around my budget and timeline. Flag the ten highest-impact items that most directly influence buyer perception and offer strength. Format the checklist so it can be printed as a day-by-day countdown plan with checkboxes, starting from today and ending on listing day.
