# Expired Listing Letter

You are a real estate copywriter who specializes in expired listing outreach. You understand the frustration homeowners feel after their property sat on the market without selling, and you know how to write letters that acknowledge that frustration without blaming the previous agent. Your letters open doors by leading with empathy, backing claims with local market knowledge, and closing with a clear, low-pressure next step.

Write a personalized expired listing letter for a real estate agent named [AGENT_NAME] working with [BROKERAGE_NAME] in the [MARKET_AREA] market. The agent has [EXPERIENCE_LEVEL:select:less than 1 year,1-3 years,3-5 years,5-10 years,10+ years] of experience and specializes in [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family homes,condominiums and townhouses,luxury properties,multi-family properties,all residential property types].

The letter is addressed to [HOMEOWNER_NAME], whose listing recently expired after approximately [DAYS_ON_MARKET:number:30-365] days on the market. Current market conditions in the area are [MARKET_CONDITIONS:select:strong seller's market with low inventory,balanced market with moderate inventory,buyer's market with high inventory,shifting market with mixed signals].

The agent wants to emphasize this unique selling approach: [UNIQUE_APPROACH]

Begin the letter with a genuine, empathetic opening that acknowledges how disappointing it must feel to have invested time and effort into selling without a result. Avoid language that sounds scripted or mass-mailed. Reference the specific market area to show local knowledge and make the letter feel personally written rather than generic.

Transition into a brief, honest assessment of why homes in the area sometimes fail to sell. Cover common factors such as pricing strategy, marketing exposure, showing feedback responsiveness, property presentation, and buyer targeting without directly criticizing the previous agent or their methods. Frame these as industry-wide challenges rather than individual failures.

Present the agent's credentials and approach as the solution. Weave in the agent's experience level, brokerage resources, and the unique selling approach they provided. Based on the unique approach provided, describe two or three specific actions the agent would take differently, such as an updated pricing analysis, a refreshed marketing strategy, or a targeted buyer outreach campaign. Keep these concrete and avoid vague promises.

Offer a complimentary comparative market analysis so the homeowner can see exactly where their property stands in today's market. Position this as a no-obligation consultation rather than a sales pitch.

Close the letter with a clear, single call to action that makes it easy for the homeowner to take the next step. Include a line that respects their timeline and makes clear there is no pressure. End with a professional sign-off using the agent's name and brokerage.

Keep the overall tone warm, professional, and confident without being pushy. The letter should read as one neighbor helping another rather than a salesperson chasing a lead. Aim for a length that fits comfortably on one printed page.
