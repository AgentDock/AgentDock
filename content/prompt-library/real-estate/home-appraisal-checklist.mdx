# Home Appraisal Checklist

I need a thorough home appraisal preparation checklist to help me present my property in the best possible light for an upcoming appraisal. The goal is to maximize the appraised value by addressing every factor that appraisers evaluate when determining what a property is worth.

The property is a [PROPERTY_TYPE:select:single-family home,townhouse,condominium,duplex,multi-family,manufactured home,luxury home] with [BEDROOMS:number:1-10] bedrooms and [BATHROOMS:number:1-8] bathrooms, totaling approximately [SQUARE_FEET:number:500-15000] square feet. It was built in [YEAR_BUILT:number:1900-2099] and sits on a lot of about [LOT_SIZE] in a [NEIGHBORHOOD_TYPE:select:suburban,urban,rural,gated community,waterfront,historic district,planned development] setting.

The appraisal is being conducted for the purpose of [APPRAISAL_PURPOSE:select:home sale,mortgage refinance,home equity line of credit,estate or divorce settlement,property tax appeal,new purchase loan,PMI removal]. I would rate the current overall condition as [CONDITION:select:excellent with recent updates,good with minor wear,fair with some deferred maintenance,needs significant work]. Recent improvements or renovations include [RECENT_IMPROVEMENTS?].

Please generate a comprehensive appraisal preparation checklist organized into the following areas. For curb appeal and exterior presentation, list every item the appraiser will observe from the street and during the exterior walkthrough, including landscaping, paint condition, roof appearance, driveway, fencing, and drainage grading. Explain which exterior items have the strongest influence on appraised value.

For interior condition and presentation, cover each room category the appraiser will evaluate. Focus on kitchens and bathrooms first since they carry the most weight in value assessments. Include flooring, walls, ceilings, lighting, fixtures, and overall cleanliness. Note which cosmetic fixes provide the best return relative to their cost.

For systems and mechanical components, list what the appraiser will check regarding HVAC age and condition, electrical panel, plumbing, water heater, and any visible safety concerns. Indicate how system age and condition factor into the final value determination.

For comparable sales preparation, explain how to research recent sales of similar properties within a half-mile radius. Describe what documentation I should prepare showing how my property compares favorably to those comps on features, upgrades, and lot characteristics.

For documentation and records, provide a list of every document I should have ready for the appraiser. Include permits for completed work, receipts for major improvements, a property survey, HOA information if applicable, and any unique features or upgrades that might not be immediately visible.

For each checklist item, indicate whether it is high, medium, or low priority based on how much it typically influences the appraised value. Flag any quick wins that can be completed in a weekend for under $500 and are likely to improve the appraisal outcome. Also note common mistakes homeowners make that can decrease appraised value, such as unpermitted additions or visible code violations.

Format the checklist so it can be printed and used as a walkthrough guide in the days leading up to the appraisal, with space to mark each item as completed.
