# Rent Increase Letter Generator

I am a [SENDER_ROLE:select:private landlord,property manager,property management company,building owner] and I need to send a rent increase notice to my tenant. The tenant's name is [TENANT_NAME] and the rental property is located at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS]. The current monthly rent is [CURRENT_RENT] and I am increasing it to [NEW_RENT], which represents a [INCREASE_TYPE:select:percentage increase of,fixed dollar increase of] [INCREASE_AMOUNT]. The new rent amount will take effect on [EFFECTIVE_DATE] and I am providing [NOTICE_PERIOD:select:30 days,60 days,90 days,other required] notice as required.

The primary reason for this rent increase is [INCREASE_REASON:select:annual market rate adjustment,increased property taxes,rising maintenance and operating costs,property improvements and upgrades completed,insurance premium increases,inflation adjustment,combination of market conditions and operating costs]. The tenant's current lease status is [LEASE_STATUS:select:month-to-month tenancy,lease expiring and this accompanies renewal offer,lease renewal with adjusted terms,periodic tenancy requiring notice]. My relationship with this tenant has been [RELATIONSHIP:select:excellent with no issues,good with minor concerns addressed,professional and standard,newer relationship still developing] during their tenancy of [TENANCY_LENGTH].

The tone I want for this letter should be [TONE:select:professional and straightforward,warm and relationship-focused,formal and legally precise,understanding while firm]. I [BENEFITS_INCLUDED:select:want to highlight recent property improvements or amenities,want to mention upcoming improvements planned,do not need to mention any improvements,want to acknowledge their good tenancy record]. Regarding payment logistics, [PAYMENT_INFO:select:payment method and due date remain the same,I am including updated payment instructions,the tenant should contact me to confirm new payment setup,automatic payment amounts will be adjusted].

Please generate a complete rent increase letter that opens with a professional greeting and clearly states the purpose of the letter. The body should specify the current rent amount and the new rent amount, state the exact effective date of the increase, explain the reason for the increase in a way that helps the tenant understand it is a reasonable business decision, and acknowledge the tenant as a valued resident. If I indicated improvements or good tenancy, incorporate that appropriately. The letter should clearly state any action the tenant needs to take, provide my contact information for questions, and close professionally. Include a signature block with space for my name, title, contact phone number, and email address. Keep the letter to one page, use clear and respectful language, and ensure the effective date and notice period are prominently stated so there is no confusion about timing.
