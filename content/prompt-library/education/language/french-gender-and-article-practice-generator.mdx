# French Gender and Article Practice Generator

Learners get taught a shortcut early, words ending in -e are usually feminine, words ending in a consonant are usually masculine, and the shortcut works often enough to feel trustworthy right up until it fails on a word they use constantly. Le problème is masculine. So is un musée and un silence, despite that -e ending everyone was told to trust. La main is feminine, breaking the pattern that almost every other -ain word is masculine. There's a second layer past gender itself too, the partitive articles du, de la, and des, which mark "some" of an uncountable noun and get skipped entirely by learners who only ever practiced le, la, un, and une.
